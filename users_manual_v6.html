OpenCourt User Manual

Everything you need to know about finding games, hosting runs, managing your squad, and building your reputation on the court.

🏀

01 — Overview

OpenCourt is the premier platform for organizing and joining pickup basketball games. Whether you are looking for a casual run or a competitive game, OpenCourt connects you with players in your area.

What You Can Do

  • Find Games — Browse available pickup games on the dashboard or live map
  • Join Games — Secure your spot with one tap; auto-waitlist if full
  • Host Games — Create your own run with custom rules, location, and entry fee
  • QR Check-In — Scan in when you arrive at the court
  • Team Matchups — Hosts can auto-generate or manually drag-and-drop players into Team Dark and Team Light, then publish the lineup for players to see
  • Track Your Reputation — Build a Reliability Score by showing up consistently
  • Log Game Stats — Record points, rebounds, assists, and more after completed games

Platform Requirements

OpenCourt works in any modern web browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge) on desktop and mobile devices. No app download required.

02 — Create an Account

Getting started on OpenCourt takes less than two minutes.

Sign Up Options

You can create your account using any of the following methods:

  • Google — Sign in with your Google account (fastest option)
  • Facebook — Sign in with your Facebook account
  • Email & Password — Create an account with your email address
💡

When signing up with email, you will receive a verification link. Click the link to activate your account before logging in.

Onboarding — Create Your Baller ID

After signing up, you will be taken to the onboarding page to set up your player profile. This is your Baller ID that other players will see.

1
Full Name

Enter your real name (e.g., "Juan dela Cruz"). Required.

2
Display Name

Choose a unique username (minimum 3 characters). This is how you appear on the court. Required.

3
Position (Optional)

Select your playing position: PG (Point Guard), SG (Shooting Guard), SF (Small Forward), PF (Power Forward), or C (Center).

4
Height (Optional)

Enter your height in feet and inches. Used for team balancing.

5
Skill Level (Optional)

Rate your skill: Casual, Competitive, or Elite. Helps hosts and the team generator.

Click "Create Baller ID" to save your profile and start using OpenCourt.

03 — Your Profile

Your profile page (accessed from the sidebar or menu) is your Player Locker. Here you can view and update all your information.

What You Can Edit

Field Description
Full Name Your real name
Display Name Your @username visible to other players
Position PG, SG, SF, PF, or C
Skill Level Casual, Competitive, or Elite
Height Your height in feet and inches

Reliability Score

Your profile displays your Reliability Score as a percentage. All new accounts start at 100%. See Section 12 for details on how this score works.

04 — Dashboard

The Dashboard is your home screen. It shows all available pickup games from the last 7 days onward, sorted by date (soonest first).

Game Cards

Each game appears as a card displaying:

  • Game title and location
  • Date and time of the game
  • Player count (e.g., "3/20 Players")
  • Format (Full Court, Full Court with Ref)
  • Skill level (Open Run, Papawis, Elite, etc.)
  • Entry fee (FREE or amount in Philippine Pesos, e.g., ₱200)

Game Card States

State Appearance Action
Open Full color card "JOIN THE RUN" button available
Joined Shows "CONFIRMED" badge Click card to view details
Cancelled Red border, reduced opacity Shows cancellation reason
Completed Green border View game stats
Waitlisted Shows amber WAITLIST badge with clock icon You are on the waitlist — spot not yet confirmed
Ended Grayed out, grayscale filter "ENDED" badge, button disabled
📌

Past games automatically appear grayed out with an "ENDED" badge. The Join button is disabled for these games.

Real-Time Updates

The Dashboard updates live as new games are posted — a "New game just posted!" toast appears at the top of the screen. Game statuses (player count, cancellation, completion) also refresh automatically without needing to reload the page.

05 — Joining a Game

Found a game you want to play? Here is how to join.

How to Join

1
Browse the Dashboard

Find a game that matches your schedule, location, and skill level.

2
Tap "JOIN THE RUN"

Click the button on the game card. If the game has house rules, you will be asked to agree before joining.

3
You Are In!

Your card now shows "CONFIRMED". You can view game details or leave the squad at any time.

Waitlist

If the game is full, tapping "JOIN THE WAITLIST" places you on the waitlist automatically. Your waitlist status is shown clearly in two places:

  • Dashboard card — an amber ⏱ WAITLIST badge replaces the green CONFIRMED badge
  • Game detail page — an amber "YOU'RE ON THE WAITLIST" banner and a "LEAVE WAITLIST" button replace the QR Check-In button

If another player leaves, you will be promoted to the main roster in the order you joined. The page updates in real time — no refresh needed.

💡

Waitlist promotions happen automatically. Once promoted, your status changes to CONFIRMED and the QR Check-In button becomes available.

Leaving a Game or Waitlist

To leave a game you have joined or a waitlist you are on:

  1. Go to the game detail page
  2. Click "Leave Squad" (if confirmed) or "Leave Waitlist" (if waitlisted)
  3. You will be removed from the roster
⚠️

Leaving games too close to start time or not showing up may affect your Reliability Score.

06 — QR Check-In

OpenCourt uses QR codes to verify that players actually show up at the court. This system keeps the community accountable.

For Players

1
Open Game Details

Navigate to the game you joined.

2
Tap "SHOW CHECK-IN QR"

A modal will appear showing your personal QR code (your Player Pass).

3
Show It to the Host

Present your screen to the host when you arrive. They will scan your code to check you in.

⚠️

The QR Check-In button is only available to confirmed players. If you are on the waitlist, you will see a "YOU'RE ON THE WAITLIST" banner instead. Wait to be promoted before arriving at the court.

For Hosts

As a host, you can check players in using the QR scanner or manually:

  • Scan Mode — Tap the scan icon in the roster section to activate your camera. Point it at a player's QR code to automatically check them in.
  • Manual Check-In — Tap the checkmark icon next to a player's name to manually mark them as checked in.

07 — My Games

The My Games page is your personal schedule. Access it from the sidebar by tapping the calendar icon.

Two Sections

🕒 Upcoming Runs (Joined Games)

A timeline view of all games you have joined. Your next game is highlighted with a Next Up badge. Each entry shows the date, time, location, player count, and entry fee.

👑 Hosted by You

All games you have created are listed here with a crown badge. Click any game to access host controls, manage the roster, or generate teams.

Click any game card to go to its detail page where you can view the full roster, check in, or manage it (if you are the host).

08 — Host a Game

Hosting a game lets you organize your own pickup run. You control the location, rules, format, and entry fee.

5-Step Game Creation Wizard

1
Location & Schedule

Enter the court name or address (required). Choose a date: Today, Tomorrow, or pick from the calendar (future dates only). Set the start time. If "Today" is selected, the time must be at least 30 minutes from now.

2
Format & Capacity

Select game format: Full Court or Full Court (with Ref). Set the maximum number of players (15–25). Choose the competition level: Open Run, Papawis, Tito Gaming, Dayo, Malakasan, or Elite.

3
Details

Add an optional description about the game and any house rules (e.g., "No hard fouls", "Call your own fouls"). House rules will be shown to players before they join.

4
Filters

Set gender restriction (Mens, Womens, or Mixed) and age range (All Ages, 12U, 14U, 17U, 18+, or 40+).

5
Cost & Publish

Review all your game details. Set the entry fee using the Free toggle or enter an amount up to ₱10,000. Tap "Publish Game" to make your game live on the dashboard.

Cost Settings

  • Free Toggle ON — Game is free to join. Shows "This game is free to join" confirmation.
  • Free Toggle OFF — Enter the entry fee in Philippine Pesos (₱). Maximum amount: ₱10,000. Numeric values only.
💡

After publishing, your game immediately appears on the Dashboard for other players to join. You will be redirected to the Dashboard.

09 — Host Controls

As the host, you have full control over your game from the game detail page.

Roster Management

Action Description
Scan QR Activate the camera scanner to check in players by scanning their QR code
Manual Check-In Tap the checkmark icon to manually mark a player as checked in
Mark Absent Tap the X icon to mark a no-show player as absent (affects their Reliability Score)
Kick from Squad Remove a player from the roster entirely
Promote Waitlist Tap "SQUAD UP" to move a waitlisted player to the main roster
Reset Status Revert a checked-in or absent player back to joined status

Game Status Actions

Complete Run

Mark the game as completed when the run is over. This enables stat tracking for all checked-in players.

Cancel Run

Cancel the game with a reason (e.g., "Rain", "Not enough players"). The cancellation reason is visible to all joined players. Cancelled games appear with a red border on the dashboard.

⚠️

Please give at least 3 hours notice when cancelling a game to respect your players' time.

10 — Team Matchups

Hosts can build and publish team lineups directly from the game detail page. Teams can be arranged automatically by the algorithm or manually using drag-and-drop. Lineups are private until the host explicitly publishes them.

Requirements

  • You must be the host of the game
  • The game status must be active (not upcoming)
  • At least 4 confirmed players must be in the roster (joined or checked-in status)

The Two Teams

⚫ Team Dark  &  ⚪ Team Light

Players are distributed between two squads — Team Dark and Team Light. Each team card shows the average OVR (Overall Rating) of its players so you can see the balance at a glance.

Building a Lineup — Host Workflow

1
Auto-Generate (optional)

Tap "Generate Teams" to let the algorithm distribute players automatically based on Skill Level (60%), Reliability Score (30%), and Height (10%). A confirmation dialog appears if a lineup already exists.

2
Drag & Drop to Adjust

Drag any player card between Team Dark, Team Light, and the Unassigned Pool. Changes are local until you save. Works on both desktop (mouse) and mobile (touch).

3
Save Lineup

Tap "Save Lineup" to persist your arrangement. An amber "Unsaved changes" banner appears whenever you have moved players but not yet saved. The Save button is disabled once the lineup is saved and unchanged.

4
Publish Matchup

Tap "Publish Matchup" to make the teams visible to all joined players. A green "Players can see this lineup" banner confirms it is live. The Squad Roster section is hidden for non-host players once teams are published.

5
Unpublish (if needed)

Tap "Unpublish" to pull back a published lineup for revisions. Players will no longer see the teams until you publish again.

Algorithm — How Teams Are Balanced

When using Auto-Generate, the algorithm scores every player and distributes them evenly:

Factor Weight Description
Skill Level 60% Player's self-reported skill (Casual, Competitive, Elite)
Reliability 30% Player's reliability percentage
Height 10% Player's height for positional balance

Balancing Process

  1. Players are grouped by position (Guard, Forward, Center)
  2. Each group is sorted by composite OVR score
  3. A snake draft distributes players between Team Dark and Team Light
  4. Each team gets an equal mix of positions

Player View

Joined non-host players see the Team Matchups section based on publish status:

State What Players See
Not published A "Team lineup is being prepared — check back soon" placeholder card
Published Team Dark and Team Light cards with full player lists and OVR scores. The Squad Roster section is also hidden to focus attention on the matchup.
💡

Player views update in real time — the moment the host publishes the lineup, all joined players see it immediately without refreshing the page.

📌

Players who are not joined to the game will not see the Team Matchups section at all, regardless of publish status.

12 — Reliability Score

Your Reliability Score represents how dependable you are as a player. It is displayed as a percentage on your profile and visible to hosts.

How It Works

  • All new accounts start at 100%
  • Your score decreases when you are marked as absent by a host (no-show)
  • Your score is used in the team generation algorithm (30% weight)
  • Higher scores signal to hosts that you are a reliable player
⚠️

Consistently not showing up to games you have joined will lower your Reliability Score. Always cancel your spot if you cannot attend.

Tips to Maintain Your Score

  • Only join games you can attend
  • Leave the squad early if your plans change
  • Always check in with the host when you arrive
  • Arrive on time — hosts may mark late arrivals as absent

13 — Game Stats

After a game is marked as completed by the host, checked-in players can log their game statistics.

How to Submit Stats

  1. Go to the completed game's detail page
  2. You will see the Stats Form (only visible if you were checked in)
  3. Enter your stats: points, rebounds, assists, steals, turnovers
  4. Submit your stats

Stats Leaderboard

Once players submit their stats, a leaderboard is displayed on the game detail page showing all participants' performances.

💡

Stats submission is only available for players who were checked in during the game. Make sure to check in with the host when you arrive!

14 — Frequently Asked Questions

Getting Started

How do I join a game?

Browse the Dashboard, find a game, and tap "JOIN THE RUN". If the game is full, you will be placed on the waitlist automatically.

What if I cannot make it to a game I joined?

Go to the game detail page and tap "Leave Squad" as soon as possible. This frees up your spot for other players and protects your Reliability Score.

What is the waitlist?

When a game is full, new players are added to a waitlist in order. If someone leaves, the first person on the waitlist is automatically promoted to the main roster.

Hosting

How do I create a game?

Tap the + button in the navigation bar or go to the Host page. Follow the 5-step wizard to set up your game details, rules, and entry fee.

Can I edit a game after publishing?

Currently, games cannot be edited after publishing. You can cancel the game and create a new one if needed.

How does QR check-in work?

Players show their QR code from the app. As the host, tap the scan button in the roster section to activate your camera and scan each player's code. You can also manually check in players by tapping the checkmark icon.

Why can't I generate teams?

Team generation requires at least 4 confirmed players (joined or checked-in status). The Auto-Generate button is disabled until that minimum is met.

Players can't see the teams I generated

Lineups are private until you explicitly Publish them. After saving your lineup, tap "Publish Matchup" to make it visible to all joined players. They will see it in real time.

Can I edit teams after publishing?

Yes. Tap "Unpublish" to hide the lineup, make your drag-and-drop adjustments, save, and publish again.

Account

How do I update my profile?

Go to the Profile page from the sidebar navigation. Update your information and tap "Save Changes".

Can I delete my account?

Yes. You can request account deletion through the app or by emailing devappaisolutions@gmail.com. All your data will be permanently deleted within 30 days. See the Data Deletion page for details.

How do I change my email?

Contact support at devappaisolutions@gmail.com for email change requests.

Troubleshooting

My check-in QR is not scanning

Make sure your screen brightness is high. Try holding the phone steady and closer to the camera. The host can also check you in manually using the checkmark button.

I was checked in but my score still dropped

Contact support with your game details. A host may have accidentally marked you absent before correcting it.
